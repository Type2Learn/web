Active learning, one keystroke at a time

Learn by typing. Build knowledge that stays.

Type2Learn turns lessons into guided recall, correction, application, and return. It is not a speed-first typing tutor, passive video library, timer around a worksheet, or points machine.

How it works

Each learning loop makes the next action clear: Read or Hear, Recall, Type or Produce, Check, Correct or Reconstruct, Apply, and Return. A meaningful completion condition replaces speed, idle time, or random clicks.

  • Feedback is specific, respectful, and gives a useful next action.
  • Answers revealed for support are followed by reconstruction.
  • Saved work, pause, refresh, and return must preserve learner progress.

Pathways

Word Builder

Structured literacy and academic word learning through sound, spelling, meaning, correction, transfer, and delayed retrieval.

Focus Sprint

Bounded academic work with one objective, a Now → Next → Done plan, protected progress, and calm re-entry.

Predictable Path

Stable lesson structure, Preview Cards, literal instruction options, declared sensory events, transitions, and Change Cards.

The initial direction is a literacy-first, audio-capable course and reusable active-learning engine. Later modules remain clearly labelled as adapted or planned until released and tested.

Learners and families

Support is a private, configurable control - not a diagnosis. Learners can choose text, spacing, contrast, audio, timer visibility, focus mode, literal instructions, sensory settings, and valid alternative input.

  • No public speed, error, disability, or focus ranking.
  • No punitive streaks or loss of progress after absence.
  • No webcam, gaze, face, emotion, or browser-surveillance feature.
  • Families receive useful learning information, not a private behavioural feed.

Schools and professionals

The intended educator view distinguishes academic evidence, correction, support used, and complete/paused/review status. It must not reduce a learner to a behaviour score or live-monitor every keystroke.

School deployment is planned and requires role-based access, a school agreement or DPA, privacy/security controls, data export/deletion, safeguarding, and accessibility readiness.

Research and co-design

Evidence informs the design; learners test the decision. Type2Learn uses transparent labels:

  • Supported: a general principle has a direct research basis.
  • Adapted: a product translation still needs testing.
  • Experimental: a hypothesis is being evaluated.
  • Community-informed: feedback from lived experience changed a decision.
  • Planned: not released and not an outcome claim.

Type2Learn is educational, not clinical or diagnostic. Learning outcome claims require defined, consented, transparent evaluation.

Privacy, terms, accessibility, security, and support

The supplied Privacy Policy and Terms are drafts pending legal review. Before publication, the organization must confirm its entity, address, launch markets, vendors, hosting, consent, retention, payment/refund model, disputes, and school agreement.

  • No learner-data sale, data brokerage, targeted advertising, or public-model training without explicit, age-appropriate permission.
  • WCAG 2.2 AA is the accessibility target: keyboard access, visible focus, semantic states/errors, captions/transcripts, zoom/reflow, and reduced motion.
  • Security direction includes least privilege, role controls, encrypted transport/storage, MFA for privileged users, audit history, backup/recovery, and incident response.
  • Support and security contact routes are pending configuration, monitoring, and governance.
