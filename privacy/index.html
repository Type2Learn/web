Before publicationCore launch assumptions

  • Type2Learn does not sell personal information and does not display third-party behavioral advertising.
  • Student content and learner telemetry are not used to train public or third-party foundation models unless a separate, explicit, age-appropriate consent process is introduced.

SummaryPlain-language privacy summary

For learners

Type2Learn helps you learn by typing. We collect only the information needed to run lessons, save progress, keep accounts safe and provide the settings you choose. Do not type passwords, home addresses, medical details or other private information into lessons. Ask a parent, teacher or trusted adult when you are unsure.

  • We minimize data. We collect account, learning, safety and support information needed to provide the service.
  • Learning data is not an advertising product. We do not sell it, use it for behavioral advertising or allow data brokers to build profiles from it.
  • Child accounts need adult or school authorization. The exact path depends on age, location and whether a school provides the account.
  • Accessibility settings stay private by default. Choosing low motion, audio feedback or literal instructions does not mean Type2Learn has diagnosed you.
  • AI is a tool, not the decision-maker. It may help create practice or feedback, but it must not make high-stakes decisions about grades, disability or discipline.
  • You can request access, correction or deletion. Parents, guardians, eligible students and schools may have additional rights.
  • Public sharing is optional. Profiles, leaderboards, certificates and social sharing should be private or limited by default for minors.
  • Security is layered, not absolute. We use safeguards, but no online service can promise zero risk.
  • We keep information only as needed. Different data types have different retention periods and school contracts may require shorter periods.
  • Privacy questions go to one place. Email support@type2learn.tech with "Privacy Request" in the subject line.

1Scope and identity

This Privacy Policy explains how personal information is collected, used, disclosed, protected, retained and deleted when a person visits type2learn.tech, creates or uses a Type2Learn account, participates through a school or organization, contacts support, joins research or testing, or interacts with a Type2Learn feature that links to this Policy.

For this draft, "Type2Learn," "we," "us" and "our" refer to the operator of type2learn.tech, a learning technology project founded by Muhammad Taha Bin Zaeem. Before launch, this paragraph must be replaced or supplemented with the registered legal entity name, business address and any local representative required by law. This Policy applies to learners, parents and guardians, teachers, school administrators, independent adult users, visitors, contributors, prospective partners and support contacts. It does not cover third-party websites or services that have their own privacy notices.

2Service and account contexts

Type2Learn is a typing-based active-learning platform. It may provide guided lessons, coding exercises, quizzes, projects, progress tracking, achievements, certificates, accessibility controls, learning pathways, integrity checks, educator dashboards and optional AI-assisted features. The privacy role of Type2Learn depends on how the service is used. In a direct account, Type2Learn generally determines why and how account information is processed. In a school-managed account, a school or district may determine the educational purpose and Type2Learn may process student information on the school's instructions under a contract or data processing agreement. Where a school creates or controls an account, the school's notices, consent choices, record-retention rules and student-rights procedures may also apply. Questions about school education records should usually be directed to the school first.

3Data we collect

3.1Account and identity information

Name or display name; username; email address; password hash or authentication token; age band or date of birth where needed for consent; country or region; language; role; school, class or organization; account status; parent or guardian contact; teacher or administrator identifiers.

3.2Learning and progress information

Courses and pathways selected; lesson attempts; typed answers; accuracy; completion; time on task; hints; quiz responses; project submissions; badges; streaks; skill estimates; certificates; educator feedback; progress history and saved preferences.

3.3Typing and interaction telemetry

Keystroke timing, corrections, pauses, error patterns, navigation actions, focus changes and anti-skip signals may be processed to provide immediate feedback, preserve lesson integrity, detect technical problems and improve learning flows. Raw input can contain personal information, so users should not type secrets unless a lesson explicitly and lawfully requires it.

3.4Accessibility and neuroinclusive preferences

Optional settings such as reduced motion, no animation, typing-sound controls, audio playback, literal instructions, font or spacing preferences, color and contrast choices, predictable transitions, focus-sprint settings and repetition preferences.

3.5Content and communications

Projects, code, essays, notes, comments, messages, portfolio content, profile biography, support emails, bug reports, survey answers and files voluntarily submitted.

3.6Voice, audio and speech features

If voice input, read-aloud or speech feedback is enabled, the service may process audio, transcripts and playback settings. The implementation should prefer on-device or transient processing and should not retain raw child voice recordings unless essential, clearly disclosed and separately authorized.

3.7Device, network and security information

IP address; browser and operating system; device type; approximate region derived from IP; language; referring page; cookie or session identifiers; timestamps; crash logs; performance information; authentication events; security alerts and abuse signals.

3.8Cookies and local storage

Session cookies, authentication tokens, accessibility preferences, consent choices, fraud-prevention identifiers and privacy-preserving analytics identifiers. Non-essential tracking must remain off until the appropriate consent has been obtained.

3.9AI feature information

Prompts, instructions, selected lesson context, generated output, feedback ratings and safety logs when an AI feature is used. The service should send only the minimum context required and remove direct identifiers where practical.

3.10Payment and transaction information

If paid plans are introduced, billing name, plan, transaction status, country and partial payment metadata may be received from a payment provider. Type2Learn should not directly store full card numbers or card security codes.

3.11School and administrator data

Rosters, class membership, teacher assignments, school identifiers, grade or age band, license information, configuration choices, support records, audit logs and records imported through approved integrations.

3.12Information from others

Parents, schools, teachers, identity providers, authorized integrations, referral partners and service providers may provide information consistent with their notices and permissions.

4Data we intentionally avoid

Type2Learn is designed around data minimization. Unless a specific feature, contract or law requires otherwise, the service should not request government identification numbers, precise home addresses, private health records, genetic information, biometric templates, payment card security codes, private social-media passwords or unrelated information about family members. Type2Learn must not infer a learner's diagnosis, disability, intelligence, mental health, socioeconomic status or protected characteristic from typing behavior. Typing patterns may be used for learning feedback or security only within documented, proportionate boundaries.

  • Do not ask students to type medical history, passwords, addresses or other secrets as sample text.
  • Do not activate microphone, camera or precise location access without a clear feature-level explanation and permission.
  • Do not create advertising audiences from student activity.
  • Do not combine school data with unrelated consumer databases.
  • Do not publish a minor's real name, school, photo or location by default.

6Legal bases

Where data-protection law requires a legal basis, Type2Learn may rely on performance of a contract, consent, compliance with legal obligations, protection of vital interests in a genuine emergency, and legitimate interests that do not override the rights of the learner. In school deployments, the school may identify the lawful educational basis and instruct Type2Learn as a processor or service provider. Consent should be specific, informed, freely given and easy to withdraw. Consent is not an appropriate basis where a learner cannot realistically refuse a non-essential practice, especially in a mandatory classroom setting. Non-essential analytics, research, public sharing and marketing should be separated from core learning access.

7Children, parents and schools

7.1Age-aware access

Type2Learn should request an age band or school context only when needed to choose a lawful account flow. It should not invite a child to evade age checks or create an adult account.

7.2Parent or guardian authorization

For a direct child account below the applicable digital-consent age, Type2Learn should provide a clear parent notice and obtain verifiable authorization before collecting more than the limited information needed to request consent or protect the service.

7.3School authorization

A school may be able to authorize an education technology service for an educational purpose under applicable law and policy. Type2Learn must use school-provided student information only for the contracted educational service, follow the school's instructions, and not repurpose it for advertising or unrelated profiling.

7.4Parent access and control

Parents or guardians may request information about a child account, review or correct information, withdraw consent and request deletion, subject to school control, legal requirements and reasonable identity verification.

7.5Student rights

Older students and eligible students may hold rights directly. Type2Learn should provide age-appropriate explanations and should not require a student to navigate legal language alone.

7.6Best interests of the child

Product choices should put child welfare, privacy, safety and agency ahead of engagement metrics. High-privacy settings should be the default, profiling should be limited, and nudges should not pressure children to disclose more data or spend money.

7.7School records

Information maintained for a school may become part of an education record. Access, correction and disclosure requests may need to be handled by the school under its student-record procedures.

Important

A school login does not automatically make every data practice lawful. Type2Learn still needs a written contract, purpose limitation, security controls, deletion terms and a clear allocation of responsibilities.

8Neurodivergence and accessibility information

Type2Learn is designed with a deep focus on neurodivergent learners and accessible interaction. A learner may choose features such as predictable paths, reduced motion, focus sprints, word-building support, speech feedback, repetition, alternative instructions, color and spacing controls or low-distraction layouts. These preferences can be sensitive because they may suggest an accessibility need. Type2Learn should treat them as private service settings, restrict access to authorized users and avoid labeling the learner with a diagnosis. The service does not provide medical diagnosis, treatment, therapy or clinical assessment. Teachers and parents should see only the information needed to support learning. A learner's preference should not be used to lower expectations, restrict opportunities, rank ability or expose the learner to stigma.

  • No diagnosis by inference. Do not translate typing speed, pauses or error patterns into a medical label.
  • Private by default. Accessibility settings should not appear on public profiles or leaderboards.
  • Learner control. Allow supports to be changed without punishment and explain consequences before changing them.
  • No dark patterns. Do not use streak loss, shame, countdowns or sensory effects to force continued participation.
  • Human review. Any suggestion that could affect teaching support should be reviewable by an authorized adult and explainable to the learner.

9AI and automated processing

Type2Learn may use AI to generate practice questions, explain concepts, summarize learner-provided material, assist educators, check formatting, suggest feedback or identify possible safety and integrity issues. AI output can be inaccurate, incomplete or biased and must be treated as assistance rather than authoritative judgment. Type2Learn should not use AI alone to diagnose a disability, assign a final grade, decide discipline, determine school admission, deny access, assess a child's future potential or make another decision with legal or similarly significant effect. When an external AI provider is used, Type2Learn should contractually restrict use of student prompts and output, disable provider training where available, minimize data, document retention and location, and provide schools with a current subprocessor list. The product must clearly label AI-generated content where a reasonable learner could mistake it for human-reviewed material. Educator review should be required for consequential content.

11Sharing and service providers

Type2Learn may disclose information only to recipients that need it for a documented purpose, including:

  • Cloud hosting, database, content delivery, authentication, security, email, support, analytics, payment and AI providers under contractual restrictions.
  • Schools, districts, teachers, parents or guardians according to account roles and permissions.
  • Professional advisers, auditors, insurers and prospective transaction parties under confidentiality and due diligence controls.
  • Authorities or other parties when required by valid law, necessary to protect a person from serious harm, or needed to defend legal rights.
  • A successor organization in a merger, financing, restructuring or asset transfer, subject to continued protection and notice where required. Before launch, Type2Learn should publish a current subprocessor list naming each provider, function, location and applicable transfer mechanism. School customers should receive advance notice of material subprocessor changes where the contract requires it.

12No sale, targeted ads or commercial profiling

Type2Learn does not sell student or child personal information. It does not use school data for targeted advertising, does not allow third-party advertising networks to track learners, and does not build commercial profiles for unrelated products. If Type2Learn later introduces sponsorships or marketing on public adult-facing pages, those activities must be separated from learner accounts, must not use school or child data, and must be described in an updated policy and consent interface before activation.

13Public profiles, leaderboards, certificates and sharing

Type2Learn may offer profiles, project showcases, achievements, leaderboards, certificates and export to professional networks. Because these features can expose identity, performance and school affiliation, public visibility must be optional and age-appropriate. For minors, the default should be private, class-only or pseudonymous. Real names, photographs, school names, exact location and contact information should not appear publicly without lawful, informed authorization. Public sharing controls should explain exactly what will be visible and should allow revocation. Certificate verification pages should expose only the minimum data needed to validate authenticity, such as a learner-approved display name, course, issue date, status and certificate identifier. QR codes should not reveal hidden personal data in the URL.

14International transfers

Type2Learn may use providers in countries other than the user's own. Where required, it should use approved transfer safeguards such as adequacy decisions, standard contractual clauses, contractual commitments, data localization or equivalent mechanisms. Schools should be able to request information about hosting regions, subprocessors and transfer safeguards. Type2Learn should avoid unnecessary geographic duplication of student information and configure regional storage when commercially and technically feasible.

16Security and incident response

Type2Learn should use layered technical and organizational safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of child and student information. Safeguards should include encryption in transit and at rest, role-based access, least privilege, multi-factor authentication for privileged users, secure development practices, dependency monitoring, audit logging, backups, vulnerability management, employee confidentiality, vendor review and incident response exercises. No security system is perfect. When a data incident occurs, Type2Learn should contain and investigate it, preserve evidence, assess affected data and risk, notify schools and authorities within contractual or legal deadlines, and communicate clearly to affected people when required. Security reports should be sent to support@type2learn.tech with the subject "Security Report." A dedicated security address and vulnerability disclosure policy should be added before public launch.

17Privacy rights and requests

  • Access or receive a copy of personal information.
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Delete information, subject to lawful exceptions and school control.
  • Restrict or object to certain processing.
  • Withdraw consent without affecting prior lawful processing.
  • Receive portable data in a commonly used format where applicable.
  • Opt out of sale, sharing, targeted advertising or certain profiling where those rights apply. Type2Learn states that it does not engage in those practices for student data.
  • Complain to a data-protection authority or education authority.
  • Request human review of a qualifying automated decision.

To submit a request, email support@type2learn.tech with "Privacy Request" in the subject line. Type2Learn may verify identity and authority, coordinate with a school, and ask for information needed to locate the account. The service should respond within the legally required period and explain any lawful denial.

18School and district controls

School customers should receive a data processing agreement or student privacy addendum covering purpose limitation, confidentiality, security, subprocessors, international transfers, incident notification, audit or assurance information, retention, deletion, ownership, educator access and termination. Administrative tools should support role-based permissions, class and roster management, data export, student deletion, visibility controls, audit history and separation between schools. Teachers should not be able to browse unrelated classes or view accessibility settings beyond their educational need. Type2Learn should provide schools with accurate responses to security and privacy questionnaires and should not describe itself as FERPA-, COPPA-, GDPR- or WCAG-certified unless an applicable certification actually exists.

19Research and de-identified data

Type2Learn may analyze aggregate or de-identified information to understand learning outcomes, accessibility and product quality. De-identification must remove direct identifiers, reduce indirect re-identification risk, limit small-group reporting and prohibit attempts to re-identify people. Identifiable research involving children, school records, disability-related information or experimental learning interventions requires a separate protocol, school or institutional approval where applicable, age-appropriate assent, parent consent where required, and a clear statement that participation does not affect grades or access. Public research should report methods and limitations honestly and should not claim that a feature treats or cures a condition unless supported by appropriate clinical evidence and regulatory authorization.

20Changes, complaints and contact

Type2Learn may update this Policy as the product, vendors or law changes. Material changes affecting child data, school data, AI use, advertising, public sharing or ownership should be announced before they take effect and should trigger renewed consent where required. Questions, complaints and requests may be sent to support@type2learn.tech.
