# Type2Learn LinkedIn upload path

**Campaign window:** Thursday 23 July–Sunday 2 August 2026  
**Time zone:** Pakistan Standard Time — PKT (UTC+05:00)  
**Publishing account:** Type2Learn LinkedIn Page  
**Campaign goal:** introduce the learning problem, show the product direction, establish trust, invite neurodivergent participation, and finish with one pinnable project overview.

This is the recommended schedule for the requested eleven-day window. It is intentionally more concentrated than Type2Learn's normal long-term cadence. The exact times are an evidence-based starting point, not a guarantee: LinkedIn's own Page guidance recommends testing times for the Page's unique audience and responding to engagement close to real time.

## Exact upload routine

### 20 minutes before

- Open the final JPEG and confirm it is the scheduled filename.
- Copy that post's caption opener and alt text from `POSTING-GUIDE.md`.
- Add the shared participation invitation and the four core hashtags.
- Confirm `contact@type2learn.tech` is being monitored.

### At the scheduled minute

- Upload the JPEG directly to the Type2Learn LinkedIn Page.
- Add the prepared alt text.
- Publish the caption as native text.
- Check the live post once on desktop and once on mobile.

### First 60 minutes

- Stay available to answer genuine comments from the Page.
- Ask a relevant follow-up instead of replying with only “thank you.”
- Never ask anyone to disclose a diagnosis or information about a child in public.
- If a founder or team member joins the discussion, their response should add real context rather than repeat the Page caption.

### After 24–48 hours

Record these LinkedIn Page metrics for every post:

- impressions;
- members reached;
- reactions, comments, and reposts;
- link clicks, when applicable;
- engagement rate.

After the first four posts, compare the **11 AM–1 PM**, **3–5 PM**, and **6:40–7:10 PM PKT** windows. If one window produces at least 25% more members reached on comparable posts, move the remaining posts in the weaker matching window to the winner while keeping at least five hours between same-day uploads.

## Timing basis

- [Buffer's 2026 LinkedIn timing study](https://buffer.com/resources/best-time-to-post-on-linkedin/) analyzed 4.8 million posts and found weekday afternoon/evening strength, with Wednesday around 4 PM, Thursday around 5 PM, Friday around 3–5 PM, Saturday around 6 PM as a secondary window, and Sunday around 10 PM.
- [Sprout Social's 2026 timing study](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/best-times-to-post-on-linkedin/) found strong late-morning through afternoon windows for education and nonprofit audiences. Its times are intended to be interpreted in the target audience's local time.
- [LinkedIn's Page posting guide](https://business.linkedin.com/content/dam/lem/business/en/advertise/linkedin-pages/lms-linkedin-page-posting-best-practices-one-pager.pdf) recommends consistent posting, testing different times for the Page's audience, and responding to engagement close to real time.
- [LinkedIn Page analytics guidance](https://www.linkedin.com/help/linkedin/answer/a564051/update-analytics-for-your-linkedin-page) identifies impressions, members reached, clicks, reactions, comments, reposts, and engagement rate as the metrics to review.
