SummaryPlain-language summary

The essentials

Use Type2Learn to learn, practice, build and demonstrate skills. Keep your account safe, submit work you are allowed to share, respect other people, and do not attack, scrape, cheat or misuse the platform. Children need the involvement or authorization of a parent, guardian or school.

  • Your account is yours. Do not share credentials, impersonate anyone or bypass age and school controls.
  • Learning requires honest participation. Do not automate typing, fake completion, manipulate rankings or submit copied work as your own.
  • You keep ownership of your work. You give Type2Learn a limited license to host and process it so the service can function.
  • AI can make mistakes. Check generated explanations and code; teachers and learners remain responsible for decisions.
  • Accessibility settings are user controls, not diagnoses. Type2Learn does not infer or certify a medical condition from typing behavior.
  • Public sharing is optional. Think carefully before sharing a profile, certificate, username, project or achievement.
  • The service will evolve. Features, courses and availability may change, but material legal or payment changes will be communicated appropriately.
  • Legal limits vary. Nothing in these Terms removes rights that applicable consumer, child-safety, education or privacy law does not allow us to remove.

8Learning integrity and anti-skip systems

Type2Learn may use interaction signals such as typing cadence, corrections, focus changes, navigation behavior, paste events and completion patterns to provide feedback and detect automated or non-participatory completion. These systems support learning integrity but are not infallible. Users may not use scripts, macros, bots, copy-paste automation, remote operators or other methods to falsely record work as completed. Educators must not treat an automated flag as conclusive proof of misconduct without review, context and an opportunity for the learner to respond.

Fairness requirement

Integrity features must account for assistive technology, alternative input methods, motor differences, language learning, intermittent connectivity and approved accommodations. Accessibility use must not be treated as cheating.
